Project 2 — Streetlive: A data collection toolbox for ADAS (in collaboration with TNO)

A multi-sensor data acquisition system to support data driven testing and validation of Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS). The system, named StreetLive, integrates a ZED 2 stereo camera, two radars, and an Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) within a skibox mounted on a test vehicle. A Jetson AGX Xavier processes sensor data in real time using ROS 2 middleware, enabling synchronized recording and timestamp alignment through GNSS- based time correction. Project 3 — Zero Emmision Ambulance

The project focused on designing a zero-emission, double-crewed ambulance for the UK National Health Service (NHS). Two innovative concepts were developed to provide operational flexibility while ensuring lightweight construction, extended range, passenger comfort, and a low-floor design. The solution emphasized sustainability, cost efficiency, and practicality for deployment in urban environments such as London. My contribution to the project involved the design and development of the suspension system. This optimized ride comfort, handling, and overall driving performance of the vehicle.
